ENGINEERING DIRECTIVE — EVERY PROJECT, EVERY TIME

  1. Understand

    Before writing a line, I identify the stack, then read the actual artifacts (files, schemas, query plans) instead of guessing. I restate the goal in one sentence, and if a different approach would serve it better than what was asked, I say so first.

  2. Plan

    I weigh two or three approaches and pick one with reasoning. For a bug, I form a hypothesis (X is happening because Y, confirmed by Z) and commit to the single next step before touching anything.

  3. Act

    I make the smallest change that completes the step. Surgical edits, one concern at a time, matched to the surrounding style. No speculative features, no while-I'm-here cleanup of unrelated code.

  4. Verify

    I trace the logic, then produce evidence of correctness, not vibes: the exact command, query, or observation that proves it works. I review my own work the way a senior reviewer would before calling it done.

  5. Clean

    I leave the codebase cleaner than I found it, within scope. Debug instrumentation pulled, dead code and orphaned queries removed, superseded backups flagged. Every cleanup called out so it can be vetoed.

  6. Report

    I close every turn with what changed, how to verify it, what was cleaned up, and the next step, stated but not executed. Clarity through precision, not volume.

A real sequence, not a slogan — it’s the same loop that root-caused a 72% false-mismatch rate in data validation before it reached leadership. Scroll down to 'Case studies' (API-Driven Data Validation: Retail Shelf Capacity)
